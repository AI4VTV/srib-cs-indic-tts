Code-Switch Text-to-Speech System for Indic Languages

Authors: Saurabh Agrawal, Raj Prakash Gohil, Vikram CM, Gopal Kumar Agrawal
Abstract: In India, several people mix their primary language with English in day-to-day conversations. For a voice assistant to interact with such users, it requires to generate code-switch audios maintaining the fluency and naturalness in its voice. Code-Switch Text-to-Speech (TTS) is a voice synthesis system in which a single speaker can speak multiple languages. State of the art TTS models works well on monolingual data but in context to India where major people mix their mother tongue with English, its essential to develop Code-Switch TTS system. Development of Code-Switch TTS for Indic languages is a challenge due to the availability of proper curated dataset. We created a bi-lingual single speaker code-switch TTS using mono-lingual Tacotron-2 architecture. In this work, we prepared the dataset by concatenating the speech samples of English and native languages, recorded by the same speaker. The concatenated samples are used to train the mono-lingual Tacotron2 with HiFi-GAN vocoder. The experiments carried out for synthesizing code-switch data in four Indian languages, i.e, Marathi, Bengali, Kannada, and Malayalam achieving above 4 MOS score for each language.
